About KW GeeseCam

Hydrocat/GeeseCam Project

Motivation

The motivation for the project was to be able to see and find out the trail conditions in the Hydrocut without having to actually go into the forest. While we can make assumptions about what the trails are like, we really can't know for sure. The project got started when one of the developers obtained a 4G modem from Worldine Canada for testing. Wordline is introducing a new 4G service.

Technology

Solar Charger

The solar charger is based on a 20W Colman solar panel from KW Surplus driving a Chinese charge controller picked up from Amazon. It's not the greatest setup, but the upgrade to an improved solar charge controller seems to allow the battery to be maintained.

The battery is a 12V deep cycle gel cell battery. The system runs on 12V.

Camera

The camera is based in a Raspberry PI W connected to a Raspberry camera. To that board is a one wire temperature sensor and a I2C light sensor. The light sensors allows the Raspberry PI to detect the amount of light and adjust the camera's exposure.

Future Direction

The current version is being upgraded to use I2C voltage monitor to check the battery voltage. We want to know when the battery is getting to low and needs some help. We've had the SD card in the Raspberry PI get chewed up when the battery drops out.

Battery, temperature, and light information will be uploaded on a regular basis to ThingSpeak to allow us to monitor the condition of the unit remotely

Future versions will be enhanced with remote RF sensor units that will collect temperature, humidity, and soil moisture readings. This should allow use to determine if the trails are muddy or not. We will allow be looking into infrared sensors to detect mud and ice directly.

We Need Help

If you are interested in participating in the project, we would use help with hardware design, printed circuit board design, software project. We are willing to work with those who have time to learn programming. Willingness to work with Linux, Raspberry PI, Ardrino, Python, and C are skills we can use/offer.

Hit our contact page and send us an email if you are interested or has skills we can use.

The Hydrocut Webcam

For the first version, we bought some parts from KW Surplus, and hiked into the forest at the Hydrocut to mount the camera and solar charger up at one of the trailheads.

This first version ran off an old car battery and a cheap solar panel. It managed to last about two weeks before the battery died and the Raspberry PI gave up.

Geese Webcam

The second version used an improved gel cell battery and was moved to one of the co-developers back yard to make getting at the device easier - no 30 min hike into the forest.

The unit lasted about a week again before the battery died. The unit is currently being upgraded and in the meantime the solar charge controller was replaced with a better model. The battery appears to be lasting.
